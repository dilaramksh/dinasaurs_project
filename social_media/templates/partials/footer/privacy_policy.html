Privacy Policy

Last Updated: March 27, 2025

Summary

At Hive Society, we prioritise your privacy and data security. This policy outlines how we collect, use, and protect your information within our platform designed specifically for university student societies.

1. Information We Collect

1.1 Personal Information

When you register for a Hive Society account, we collect:

  • Full name
  • University email address
  • Student ID number
  • Course/degree information
  • Year of study
  • Graduation year of study

1.2 Society Membership Information

When you join or create societies, we collect:

  • Society membership status
  • Society role or position
  • Participation in society events
  • Content contributions to society pages

1.3 Technical Information

We automatically collect:

  • IP address
  • Browser type and version
  • Device information
  • Access dates and times
  • Pages visited
  • Actions performed on the platform

2. How We Use Your Information

2.1 Core Platform Functionality

We use your information to:

  • Create and maintain your user account
  • Enable society membership management
  • Facilitate event registration and attendance tracking
  • Support society communications and content sharing
  • Verify university affiliation and eligibility

2.2 Platform Improvement

We use anonymised and aggregated data to:

  • Analyze platform usage patterns
  • Identify and fix technical issues
  • Develop new features based on user needs
  • Optimize platform performance

2.3 Administrative Oversight

University administrators may access certain information to:

  • Verify compliance with university policies
  • Review and approve society registration
  • Investigate reported policy violations
  • Generate aggregated reports on society activities

3. Data Sharing and Disclosure

3.1 Within the Platform

Your information may be visible to:

  • Society leaders of societies you join (limited to membership information)
  • Other society members (limited to name and membership status)
  • University administrators (for oversight purposes)

3.2 Third-Party Services

We use select third-party services for:

  • Hosting infrastructure and cloud storage
  • Email notifications
  • Analytics (anonymised data only)

All third-party providers are contractually obligated to maintain strict data protection standards compatible with our policy.

3.3 Legal Requirements

We may disclose information when:

  • Required by applicable law or regulation
  • Necessary to respond to legal process or government requests
  • Essential to protect our rights, safety, or property
  • Required to investigate potential violations of our terms of service

4. Data Security

We implement comprehensive security measures including:

  • Encryption of personal data in transit and at rest
  • Regular security audits and vulnerability assessments
  • Access controls and authentication requirements
  • Secure development practices and code reviews
  • Regular backups and disaster recovery procedures
  • Staff training on data protection and security protocols

While we take extensive measures to protect your information, no digital platform can guarantee absolute security. We continuously update our security practices to address emerging threats.

5. Your Privacy Rights

As a Hive Society user, you have the right to:

  • Access your personal information
  • Correct inaccurate data
  • Request deletion of your account and associated data
  • Restrict certain processing of your information
  • Export your data in a machine-readable format
  • Object to specific uses of your information
  • Withdraw consent where processing is based on consent

To exercise these rights, please contact our privacy team at contact@hivesociety.com.

6. Cookies and Similar Technologies

Hive Society uses:

  • Essential cookies: Required for core platform functionality
  • Functional cookies: Remember your preferences and settings
  • Analytical cookies: Help us understand platform usage patterns

You can manage cookie preferences through your browser settings. Disabling essential cookies may impact platform functionality.

7. Data Retention

We retain your information:

  • For active accounts: As long as your account remains active
  • For inactive accounts: Up to 2 years after last activity
  • For society content: As long as the society remains active on the platform
  • For technical logs: Up to 180 days

After these periods, personal data is either anonymized or securely deleted, except where longer retention is required by law or for legitimate institutional purposes.

8. Children's Privacy

Hive Society is designed for university students and is not intended for individuals under 18 years of age. We do not knowingly collect personal information from individuals under 18. If we discover we have collected information from a person under 18, we will delete that information promptly.

9. International Data Transfers

Hive Society primarily operates within the university's infrastructure located in the United Kingdom. However, some third-party service providers may process data in other countries. When data is transferred internationally, we ensure appropriate safeguards are in place to protect your information in compliance with applicable data protection laws.

10. Policy Updates

We may update this privacy policy periodically to reflect changes in our practices, platform features, or legal requirements. Significant changes will be communicated through:

  • Email notifications to registered users
  • Notices on the platform login page
  • Platform notifications for active users

The "Last Updated" date at the top of this policy indicates when it was most recently revised.

11. Contact Information

If you have questions, concerns, or requests regarding this privacy policy or our data practices, please contact us:

Privacy Team
Hive Society
Email: contact@hivesociety.com

For urgent concerns, you may also contact the university's Data Protection Officer at contact@hivesociety.com.

By using Hive Society, you acknowledge that you have read and understood this Privacy Policy.
